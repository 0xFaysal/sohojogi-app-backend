## **1.1 What is SHOJOGI?**

SHOJOGI is a decentralized B2B2C e-commerce and supply chain ecosystem designed to digitize the traditional local "
Baki" (due) system in Bangladesh. It bridges the gap between offline trust and digital commerce by connecting local
consumers, neighborhood shop owners, and wholesale dealers through a single unified platform.

| Core Value Proposition SHOJOGI empowers the unbanked and underbanked population with a portable digital financial identity built on their daily Baki habits, while equipping neighborhood shops with modern data, digital ledgers, and automated B2B supply chain connectivity — without stripping them of their autonomy. |
|:---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

## **1.2 Motivation**

In Bangladesh, daily commerce for the middle and lower-middle class relies heavily on informal, relationship-based store
credit locally known as Baki. This system has three critical problems:

• For Consumers: A customer's repayment history at one shop is untrackable and means nothing at another. In emergencies,
this limits purchasing power to just the immediate neighborhood.

• For Merchants: Poorly maintained credit ledgers lead to bad debts. Stockouts occur due to lack of automated inventory
tracking. Manual supply chain interactions with dealers are inefficient.

• For Dealers: Inefficient traditional distribution models with limited visibility into actual hyper-local retail
demand.

## **1.3 Three Illustrative Scenarios**

### **Scenario 1 — The Trust Localization Problem**

Rahim has bought groceries on credit from his local shop for 5 years, always paying on time. During a medical emergency,
he needs medicine from a pharmacy near the hospital in a different area. The new pharmacy does not know Rahim and
refuses credit — his 5 years of perfect repayment history is worthless outside his neighborhood.

SHOJOGI Solution: Rahim uses his Portable Digital Baki score, allowing the new pharmacy to verify his trustworthiness
instantly and provide the medicine on credit.

### **Scenario 2 — The Merchant's Capital Trap**

Jamal, a kind-hearted shopkeeper, gives "due" easily to familiar faces without a strict tracking system. Customers delay
payments or forget, and Jamal loses track. His capital gets stuck in uncollected debts, preventing him from restocking
inventory.

SHOJOGI Solution: Jamal uses the Digital Merchant Ledger to track all dues, send SMS/WhatsApp reminders, and build
customer credit profiles, securing his working capital.

### **Scenario 3 — The Retailer-Wholesale Disconnect**

Jamal's shop experiences a sudden surge in demand for a specific brand of rice. He does not realize he is running low
until stock is completely empty. He then has to close his shop to visit the wholesale market or wait days for a dealer's
distribution van.

SHOJOGI Solution: Automated 30% low-stock alerts notify Jamal beforehand. He instantly places an order through the B2B
Wholesale Sourcing network.

## **2.1 Competitor Overview**

### **ShopUp (Mokam & REDX)**

A major B2B commerce and logistics platform connecting mills and manufacturers to small neighborhood shops. Business
model: B2B platform embedding commerce, logistics (REDX), and financing. Founded 2015\.

Weakness relative to SHOJOGI: ShopUp focuses purely on the supply chain (backend) and does not assist the shop in
selling to local consumers or digitizing the Baki lending system.

### **Chaldal**

A leading B2C hyper-local grocery and daily essentials e-commerce platform. Business model: Direct-to-Consumer (B2C)
retail with centralized dark stores and in-house delivery fleet. Founded 2013\.

Weakness relative to SHOJOGI: Chaldal competes directly with neighborhood shops rather than empowering them. It relies
on upfront payments and does not cater to the lower-middle-class need for daily credit.

### **TallyKhata**

A popular digital bookkeeping and fintech app for local merchants to record daily sales, expenses, and dues. Business
model: Freemium SaaS & Fintech monetizing via the TallyPay digital wallet. Founded 2020\.

Weakness relative to SHOJOGI: TallyKhata is a passive internal record-keeping tool. It tracks Baki for the merchant but
does not convert that data into a portable trust metric for the consumer, nor does it generate new consumer traffic.

## **2.3 Benchmark Scenario Analysis**

### **Scenario 1 — Competitor lacks a feature present in SHOJOGI**

Case: TallyKhata lacks a B2C Hyper-local E-commerce Storefront, a core feature provided by SHOJOGI.

Strategic Impact: This limitation puts TallyKhata at a distinct disadvantage, restricting it to being a purely passive,
internal record-keeping utility. While merchants can track dues on TallyKhata, they are entirely reliant on physical
foot traffic for sales. By integrating a B2C storefront, SHOJOGI actively drives new online customers and direct revenue
to the local shop — transitioning from a basic ledger into a vital business growth engine.

### **Scenario 2 — Competitor has a feature excluded from SHOJOGI**

Case: Chaldal utilizes Centralized Warehousing and an In-house Delivery Fleet, which SHOJOGI intentionally does not
implement.

Competitor's Strength: This model places Chaldal in a strong position regarding quality control, standardized inventory,
and guaranteed delivery timelines.

Why We Excluded It: Operating dark stores and delivery fleets is massively capital-intensive. More crucially, this
approach actively competes against our target audience — local neighborhood merchants. Instead of replacing them,
SHOJOGI empowers existing local shops as decentralized inventory hubs, maintaining an operation that is asset-light and
highly scalable.

### **Scenario 3 — Feature unique to SHOJOGI**

Case: The Portable Consumer Baki Score is an innovation exclusive to SHOJOGI. No competitor — ShopUp, Chaldal, or
TallyKhata — does anything like it. By offering a verifiable, portable digital identity built on previous repayment
history, SHOJOGI grants the unbanked lower-middle class cross-store purchasing power — a tangible financial freedom that
guarantees high consumer adoption, real-world utility, and deep long-term ecosystem loyalty.

back.

| The Correct Approach Minimum viable registration first. Get the merchant inside the app. Let them experience the value — browse the platform, set up their shop profile, add products, see how the ledger works. | 6\. Payment Flow & Merchant Earnings |
|:------------------------------------:|

## **6.1 Core Concept — Escrow \+ Settlement Model**

When a consumer pays via SSLCommerz, the money goes to SHOJOGI's bank account first — not directly to the merchant.
SHOJOGI holds it temporarily (escrow), then releases it to the merchant after confirming delivery. This is how every
marketplace works: Daraz, Shohoz, Amazon.

| 1 | Consumer Consumer pays BDT 500 via SSLCommerz Consumer completes checkout. SSLCommerz processes the card or mobile banking payment. BDT 500 leaves the consumer's account. |
|:-:|:---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

| 2 | SSLCommerz → SHOJOGI Money arrives in SHOJOGI's bank account SSLCommerz deposits into SHOJOGI's registered merchant bank account. In the database this is recorded as a pending settlement for the merchant — money exists but not yet released. |
|:-:|:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

| 3 | Merchant Merchant confirms order delivered Merchant marks order as Ready or Delivered in the app. This triggers the settlement process. Consumer may also confirm receipt. |
|:-:|:---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

| 4 | SHOJOGI platform Platform deducts commission, credits merchant balance SHOJOGI deducts its 5% commission and credits the remaining 95% to the merchant's SHOJOGI earnings balance in the database. Example: BDT 500 payment → BDT 25 commission → BDT 475 credited to merchant balance. |
|:-:|:----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

| 5 | Merchant → Bank (manual) Merchant requests withdrawal to their bank Merchant opens Earnings screen, sees balance, taps Withdraw. Admin manually transfers to merchant's registered bank account within 48 hours. Earnings balance decremented to zero. |
|:-:|:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

## **6.2 Cash on Delivery Flow**

COD is simpler — no SSL payment involved. Consumer pays physical cash to the merchant on pickup or delivery. Commission
collected differently.

• Step 1: Consumer places COD order. No payment taken. Order status: Pending COD. Merchant notified.

• Step 2: Merchant prepares and delivers. Consumer pays cash on pickup. Merchant taps Mark delivered \+ cash received.

• Step 3: SHOJOGI records BDT 500 as completed COD transaction. Creates a commission due entry — merchant owes SHOJOGI
BDT 25\.

• Step 4: Commission collected via offset — future SSL payments to the merchant are reduced by outstanding COD
commission. Or shown as commission due balance in merchant dashboard.

• Step 5: Merchant keeps the physical cash. No bank transfer from SHOJOGI to merchant needed for COD orders.

## **6.3 Merchant Earnings Ledger (MongoDB)**

Each merchant has a transactions collection in MongoDB recording every credit and debit. The available balance is
computed by summing all credits minus all debits.

| DB Field       | Type     | Values                           | Required | Notes                                      |
|:---------------|:---------|:---------------------------------|:---------|:-------------------------------------------|
| **merchantId** | ObjectId | —                                | Yes      | Reference to merchant user                 |
| **orderId**    | ObjectId | —                                | If order | Reference to order                         |
| **type**       | String   | credit / withdrawal / commission | Yes      | Type of transaction                        |
| **amount**     | Number   | BDT value                        | Yes      | Positive always; type determines direction |
| **status**     | String   | pending / settled                | Yes      | Pending until confirmed                    |

readable, (4) Card looks authentic.

• Approve: one click. Status \= verified. Push notification sent to consumer. Admin ID and timestamp logged.

• Reject: selects reason from dropdown (Image too blurry / Name mismatch / DoB mismatch / Back image missing / Card
appears edited / Please retake in better lighting) or types custom reason. Notification sent to consumer with the reason

| 10\. UX Best Practices for Registration Forms |
|:---------------------------------------------:|

|   | Multi-step with always-visible progress bar Never show all fields on one screen. Max 5 fields per step. The progress bar showing Step 2 of 5 reduces abandonment by giving users a sense of control and showing how little is left. Users are significantly more likely to complete a 5-step form than a single long scrolling form. |
|:--|:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

|   | Auto-save after every step Save to AsyncStorage (React Native) locally after each step completes. Show Draft saved confirmation. If app closes mid-registration, user resumes where they left off. Critical for merchants who need to gather physical documents. |
|:--|:-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

|   | Inline real-time validation — never batch errors at Submit Validate each field on blur (when user leaves the field). Green tick for valid, red message for invalid immediately. Showing 10 error messages after clicking Submit is the most frustrating registration UX pattern — avoid it completely. |
|:--|:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

|   | OTP verification early — never at the end Verify the phone number in Step 2, not after the entire form is filled. Discovering an OTP failure after 10 minutes of data entry is the most common cause of registration abandonment. |
|:--|:----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

|   | Document upload guidance image For NID and license photo uploads, show a sample reference image directly beside the upload box: card on dark surface, all 4 corners visible, good lighting. This reduces blurry and rejected submissions significantly. |
|:--|:--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

|   | Save and continue later for merchant document step Because documents require physical items, merchants need to be able to exit after completing basic info and return when they have their NID and trade license ready. Show Resume registration prominently on login screen if a draft exists. |
|:--|:------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

|   | GPS button prominently at the top of address forms Use my current location placed at the very top fills Division \+ District \+ Upazila in one tap via GPS. Most mobile users will grant location permission for this convenience — 3 dropdowns done instantly. |
|:--|:----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

|   | Merchant approval — set clear time expectations Immediately after submit show: Expected approval: within 48 hours. Send an SMS confirmation instantly so the merchant knows the form was received. Never leave them wondering if the submission went through. |
|:--|:--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

|   | Rejection must be specific and resubmit only the flagged section NID image is blurry — retake in better lighting is good. Documents invalid is bad. Only show the rejected section to fix — never make the merchant redo the entire form. Pre-fill name and dates, reset only the images. |
|:--|:------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

|   | Pre-fill wherever possible Username auto-suggested from first+last name. Postal code auto-fills from post office. Shop phone pre-filled from owner phone. Division auto-detected from GPS. Every pre-fill removes one friction point. |
|:--|:--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

|   | Bangla field labels with English placeholder examples Labels in Bangla for local users, placeholder example text in both languages. Serves lower-literacy users while keeping the form readable for portfolio reviewers and educated users simultaneously. |
